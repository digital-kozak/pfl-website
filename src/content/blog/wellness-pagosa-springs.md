# Wellness in Pagosa Springs: Beyond the Hot Springs — Spas, Yoga & Mindfulness

Pagosa Springs is famous for its geothermal hot springs — and rightfully so. But the town's wellness offerings extend far beyond soaking in mineral water. From therapeutic massage and yoga studios to meditation in mountain meadows and holistic healing practices, Pagosa provides a complete wellness ecosystem for visitors seeking relaxation, rejuvenation, and restoration.

Whether you're recovering from a day of skiing, seeking stress relief, or simply want to prioritize your wellbeing during vacation, this guide covers the best wellness experiences Pagosa has to offer beyond the hot springs.

## Massage Therapy & Bodywork

### The Springs Resort Spa

**Location:** The Springs Resort & Spa (15 minutes from the cabin)  
**Services:** Full-service spa with massage, facials, body treatments  
**Booking:** Recommended in advance, especially weekends

The Springs Resort offers the most comprehensive spa experience in Pagosa. Their treatment rooms overlook the San Juan River, and many services can be combined with hot springs soaking for the ultimate relaxation experience.

**Massage Options:**
- **Swedish massage** — Classic relaxation massage
- **Deep tissue** — For chronic tension and muscle recovery
- **Hot stone** — Heated stones melt tension away
- **Sports massage** — Perfect after skiing or hiking
- **Couples massage** — Side-by-side treatments
- **Prenatal massage** — For expecting mothers

**Body Treatments:**
- **Body wraps** — Detoxifying and hydrating
- **Scrubs** — Exfoliation with mineral salts
- **Facials** — Using organic, natural products

**Why Choose The Springs:** The combination of professional therapists, beautiful treatment rooms, and immediate access to the hot springs creates a world-class spa experience.

**Price Range:** $120 - $250 for massage; packages available

---

### Overlook Hot Springs Spa

**Location:** Downtown Pagosa Springs  
**Services:** Massage therapy, hot springs soaking  
**Booking:** Reservations recommended

Overlook Hot Springs offers massage therapy in addition to their rooftop soaking tubs. Their massage rooms maintain the same Victorian charm as the rest of the facility.

**Services:**
- Swedish and deep tissue massage
- Hot stone therapy
- Couples massage available
- Combination packages with soaking

**Why Choose Overlook:** The intimate setting and combination with their unique rooftop tubs makes for a special experience.

**Price Range:** $80 - $180

---

### Healing Waters Resort & Spa

**Location:** Pagosa Springs  
**Services:** Massage therapy, mineral pool soaking  
**Booking:** Walk-ins often available

Healing Waters offers massage therapy alongside their 100% natural mineral pools. Their therapists specialize in therapeutic and relaxation massage.

**Services:**
- Therapeutic massage
- Relaxation massage
- Combination with mineral pool access

**Why Choose Healing Waters:** Budget-friendly option with authentic mineral water soaking.

## Wellness-Focused Activities

### Forest Bathing (Shinrin-Yoku)

The Japanese practice of forest bathing — immersing yourself in the forest atmosphere — is perfectly suited to Pagosa's surroundings:

**How to Practice:**
- Slow, mindful walking on forest trails
- Engaging all five senses
- No destination — just being present
- Deep breathing of phytoncides (forest air)

**Best Spots:**
- San Juan National Forest trails
- Williams Creek area
- Piedra River trails

**Benefits:**
- Reduced stress hormones
- Lower blood pressure
- Improved immune function
- Mental clarity

---

## Wellness Tips for Visitors

### Altitude Considerations

At 7,000+ feet, wellness practices may feel different:
- **Hydrate more** — Drink extra water
- **Ease into exercise** — Allow time to acclimate
- **Listen to your body** — Rest when needed
- **Protect from sun** — UV is intense at altitude

### Combining with Hot Springs

The ultimate Pagosa wellness experience combines multiple modalities:
1. **Morning yoga** or meditation
2. **Massage** or bodywork
3. **Hot springs soaking**
4. **Healthy meal**
5. **Rest and relaxation**

### Booking Tips

- **Book spa services in advance** — Especially weekends and holidays
- **Ask about packages** — Many spas offer combination deals
- **Check for visitor specials** — Some places offer discounts for tourists
- **Consider mobile services** — Some therapists come to your cabin

### What to Bring

- **Comfortable clothes** for yoga and relaxation
- **Water bottle** — Stay hydrated
- **Journal** — For reflection
- **Walking shoes** — For forest bathing
- **Layers** — Mountain weather changes quickly
