# Best Restaurants in Pagosa Springs: Complete Local Dining Guide 2025

After a day of skiing at Wolf Creek or soaking in the hot springs, nothing hits the spot like a great meal. Pagosa Springs may be a small mountain town, but its dining scene punches well above its weight with over 40 restaurants, cafes, and eateries to choose from. From craft breweries and BBQ joints to authentic Mexican cuisine and cozy breakfast spots, Pagosa offers culinary experiences that rival much larger destinations.

The town's location — surrounded by ranching country and close to the New Mexico border — creates a unique food culture that blends mountain comfort with Southwestern flavors. Local chefs take advantage of Colorado beef, fresh produce, and chile peppers that would make any New Mexican proud.

In this comprehensive dining guide, we'll explore the best restaurants Pagosa Springs has to offer, organized by cuisine type and dining style. Whether you're looking for a romantic dinner, family-friendly fare, or the perfect après-ski burger, you'll find it here.

## Craft Breweries & Casual Dining

### Riff Raff Brewing Company

**Hours:** Daily 11am-9pm  
**Phone:** (970) 264-6274

**The Scene:** Locals' favorite brewpub housed in a beautifully restored historic downtown building

Riff Raff has become synonymous with Pagosa's craft beer scene. Located in a building that dates back to the early 1900s, the brewery maintains original architectural features while creating a warm, welcoming atmosphere. The dog-friendly patio is perfect on sunny afternoons, and live music on weekends draws both locals and visitors.

**Must-Try Menu Items:**
- **Trout Creek Amber** — their flagship brew, named after a local stream
- **Green Chile Cheeseburger** — Colorado classic done right with locally sourced beef
- **Beer-battered fish and chips** — using their own ale in the batter
- **Bison chili** — hearty mountain fare perfect after a day outdoors

**Why We Love It:** Beyond the excellent beer and food, Riff Raff is committed to sustainable brewing practices. They were among the first in Colorado to install solar panels and use innovative water conservation techniques. Plus, it's just 15 minutes from our [luxury cabin](/the-cabin), making it an easy dinner destination.

**Pro Tip:** Their downtown location makes it perfect for a stroll along the riverwalk after dinner.

## Dining Tips for Visitors: Making the Most of Pagosa's Food Scene

### Seasonal Considerations

| Season | Dining Notes |
|--------|--------------|
| **Winter** | Some restaurants reduce hours; call ahead during off-peak weeks |
| **Summer** | Patio dining is prime real estate — arrive early for outdoor tables |
| **Holidays** | Book reservations well in advance, especially Christmas through New Year |
| **Mud Season** (April-May) | Slower pace, shorter waits, occasional closures for vacation |
| **Fall** | Harvest specials, pleasant patio weather |

### Reservation Strategies

- **Weekends:** Book 2-3 days ahead for nicer restaurants like Meander
- **Meander:** Reservations essential — book online at meandereatery.com
- **Large groups (6+):** Always call ahead
- **Walk-ins:** Most casual spots accept walk-ins, but expect waits during peak times

### Takeout and Cabin Dining

Sometimes after a long day of skiing or exploring, you simply want to enjoy dinner at your **private cabin** with the hot tub waiting. Excellent takeout options include:

- **Rosie's Pizzeria** — pick up on your way home
- **Tequila's** — family feast packages perfect for groups
- **Square Top BBQ** — grab smoked meats by the pound
- **Papa Murphy's** — take-and-bake convenience

Our [fully equipped kitchen](/the-cabin) makes cabin dining easy and enjoyable. The large dining table seats 8 comfortably, and the outdoor grill extends your cooking options during warmer months.
