Things To Do

Your Guide

Experience the Best of
Pagosa Springs

Nestled in the heart of the San Juan Mountains, Pagosa Springs offers year-round adventures for every type of traveler. From legendary powder skiing to soothing natural hot springs, world-class fishing to breathtaking hiking trails—your mountain escape is just the beginning.

15 Minutes • Year-Round

The Springs Resort & Spa

Home to the world's deepest geothermal hot spring, The Springs Resort offers 24 pools ranging from 83°F to 114°F. Soak in mineral-rich waters while enjoying panoramic views of the San Juan Mountains—truly magical in winter when snow falls around you.

November - April

Fall & Winter Adventures

When warm sunny days give way to beautiful changing leaves and dreamy snowfalls, discover the charms of Pagosa Springs. From thrilling skiing to peaceful winter hikes, cold weather brings endless adventure.

23 Miles • Nov-Apr

Wolf Creek Ski Area

"The Most Snow in Colorado" with over 465 inches annually. Ski and snowboard where the most snow falls in the state, far away from crowds and lift lines.

5-30 Minutes • Dec-Mar

Snowshoe & XC Ski

Explore pristine winter landscapes on 50km of groomed trails. Free and dog-friendly paths through Reservoir Hill and Coyote Hill Nordic Loops for all skill levels.

15-45 Minutes • Dec-Feb

Ice Fishing

Some of the best ice fishing in southwestern Colorado. Find rainbow trout and yellow perch at Echo Reservoir, or kokanee salmon at Williams Creek Reservoir. License required.

Downtown • Dec-Feb

Skating Ponds

A favorite winter pastime for the whole family. The ponds behind River Center are maintained throughout winter. Completely free when ice is at least four inches thick.

5-30 Minutes • Dec-Mar

Sledding & Tubing

Hours of endless fun for adults and kids alike. Free local favorites include Reservoir Hill in downtown Pagosa and the parking area near Lobo Overlook on Wolf Creek.

Local • Nov-Mar

Horse Drawn Sleigh Rides

The romance of the Rockies and nostalgia of a horse-drawn sleigh. Your 30-minute adventure includes a warm beverage and scenic ride along the San Juan River and majestic mountains.

Local • Dec-Mar

Snowmobiling

Feel the rush of buzzing through the snow in the Rockies. Two-hour guided tours through the beautiful backcountry of the San Juan Mountains.

Local • Year-Round

Hot Air Balloon Rides

Soar high above hidden canyons and valleys. Experience the beauty of the Rocky Mountains from 120 feet in the air—a truly unique and magical perspective.

5-45 Minutes • Sep-Oct

Fall Hiking & Biking

Endless miles of scenic trails surrounded by 3 million acres of wilderness. Experience wildlife viewing and spectacular changing foliage in the San Juan National Forest.

Various • Sep-Oct

Scenic Fall Drives

Some of the best scenic drives in Colorado, surrounded by 3 million acres of wilderness and national forest. Experience golden aspens and breathtaking mountain vistas.

May - September

Spring & Summer Adventures

Nothing says spring and summer like a lazy float down the San Juan River. Whether you love the rush of rapids, 4×4 trails to mountain peaks, or exploring ancient archaeological sites, Pagosa Springs delivers unforgettable sunny season adventures.

Downtown • May-Sep

River Rafting & Tubing

Experience whitewater thrills on the Piedra River or take a leisurely float through the Pagosa Springs Whitewater Park. Prime rafting months are May and June.

On Property • May-Sep

Horse Drawn Wagon Rides

Traditional family fun at The Springs Resort. Meet 2,000-pound draft horses, enjoy a beverage, and take a scenic 30-minute ride along the San Juan River with mountain views.

Various • May-Oct

ATV & 4×4 Trails

Armed with a map and your spirit of adventure, explore trails through San Juan Forest. Cross creeks, climb rocky grades, and wind through narrow forest trails.

15 Minutes • May-Oct

Horseback Riding

Experience 3 million acres of San Juan Forest the way it was trekked decades ago—on horseback. Gentle rides through extraordinary backcountry wilderness.

45 Minutes • Year-Round

Chimney Rock National Monument

A sacred site and celestial observatory of the Ancestral Puebloans from 1,000+ years ago. Explore 200 ancient homes, petroglyphs, and great kivas with dramatic panoramic views.

Various • May-Oct

Rock Climbing

A haven for rock climbing. Try Piedra River Canyon or world-famous Penitente Canyon with 300+ routes. Known worldwide as both a great place to learn and a premiere destination.

Local • May-Oct

Championship Mountain Golf

Tee off at over 7,000 feet surrounded by Ponderosa Pines and Aspen. Hit your drive farther than ever thanks to the altitude. After golf, enjoy unlimited healing mineral soaks.

5-45 Minutes • May-Oct

Hiking & Backpacking

3 million acres of national forest and wilderness provide nearly endless opportunities. Discover immediate solitude, amazing scenery, and endless trails with a backcountry Hut System.

Various • May-Oct

Waterfalls

Four magnificent, accessible waterfalls in the Pagosa Springs area. Each is a unique experience— we highly suggest visiting them all. Springtime provides the best runoff viewing.

1-3 Hours • May-Oct

Scenic Train Rides

Step back into the Old West on the Cumbres & Toltec, Durango & Silverton, or Rio Grande Scenic Railroad. Each offers unique experiences and breathtaking Rocky Mountain views.

Downtown • Year-Round

Theatre & Performing Arts

Year-round professional theatre at Thingamajig Theatre Company, plus community theatre with Curtains Up Pagosa celebrating 29 years. There's a show for everyone!

Various • May-Sep

Boating, Kayaking & SUP

Rivers, lakes and reservoirs provide endless boating options—from kayaking and whitewater rafting to canoeing, motorized boating, SUP and jetskiing.

10 Minutes • May-Sep

Echo Canyon Reservoir

A beautiful lake just south of Pagosa perfect for fishing, paddle boarding, canoeing, kayaking, and even launching a pontoon boat.

45 Minutes • May-Sep

Williams Creek Reservoir

A gorgeous lake nestled against the San Juan Mountains. Perfect for hiking, fishing, and scenic picnics—one of Pagosa's most accessible, scenic locales.

Downtown • May-Oct

Disc Golf

An amazing 18-hole course on Reservoir Hill with fairways carved through Ponderosa, Pinons, and Scrub Oak. Dogs and kids welcome. Wear good shoes and bring water.

Various • May-Oct

Continental Divide 4×4 Trails

Explore ghost towns, gold mines, and mountain peaks. Cross the Continental Divide at Wolf Creek Pass, spot elk herds, and see blankets of wildflowers on the Elwood Pass Trail.

Downtown • Year-Round

Reservoir Hill Park

An outdoor treasure for hiking, mountain biking, snowshoeing, and cross-country skiing. Also home to an 18-hole disc golf course right next to the resort.

Downtown • Year-Round

River Center Park

Situated on the San Juan River with river access, two ponds for fishing and ice skating, plus tables and barbecues for picnics. A perfect place to relax on park benches.

45 Minutes • May-Sep

Navajo State Park

Colorado's answer to Lake Powell. Navajo Reservoir extends 20 miles into New Mexico with 15,000 surface acres for boating, fishing, camping, and water sports.
