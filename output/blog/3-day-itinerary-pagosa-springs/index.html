3-Day Pagosa Springs Itinerary: The Perfect Mountain Getaway

February 19, 2026 • By Pagosa Forest Lodge

3-Day Pagosa Springs Itinerary: The Perfect Mountain Getaway

Print this guide or save it to your phone for easy reference during your trip

Overview

This carefully crafted 3-day itinerary balances adventure and relaxation, giving you a taste of everything that makes Pagosa Springs special — hot springs, mountain scenery, great food, and unique experiences. It’s designed to minimize driving while maximizing enjoyment.

Best For: First-time visitors, couples, small families
Season: Year-round (activities adjust by season)
Base: Pagosa Forest Lodge — your private mountain retreat

Day 1: Arrival & Hot Springs

Morning/Afternoon: Arrive & Settle In

12:00 PM — Check In at Pagosa Forest Lodge

  • Get the tour of your 4-bedroom cabin
  • Unpack and explore the 5-acre property
  • Take in the mountain views from the deck

1:00 PM — Lunch in Town

  • Option 1: The Junction Restaurant (classic diner, huge portions)
  • Option 2: Root House Coffee Shop (sandwiches and coffee)
  • Option 3: Pagosa Baking Company (pastries and light lunch)

Afternoon: First Hot Springs Experience

3:00 PM — The Springs Resort & Spa

  • 15 minutes from the cabin
  • 24 pools ranging from 83°F to 114°F
  • Try the different pools: Lithia (hottest), San Juan (views), Terraced (Instagram-worthy)
  • Stay for 2-3 hours

What to Bring:

  • Swimsuit
  • Flip-flops
  • Robe (or rent one)
  • Water bottle
  • Phone for photos

Pro Tip: Weekday afternoons are least crowded.

Evening: Dinner & Relaxation

7:00 PM — Dinner at Riff Raff Brewing

  • Local craft beer and elevated pub food
  • Try the Green Chile Cheeseburger
  • Dog-friendly patio

9:00 PM — Back at the Cabin

  • Private hot tub under the stars
  • Fire pit (weather permitting)
  • Plan tomorrow’s adventures

Day 2: Mountain Adventure

Morning: Scenic Drive & Waterfall

8:00 AM — Breakfast at the Cabin

  • Cook in the fully equipped kitchen
  • Or grab breakfast burritos from Bruin Coffee

9:30 AM — Drive to Treasure Falls

  • 25 minutes from the cabin
  • 0.8-mile round-trip hike
  • 105-foot waterfall
  • Easy trail, great for all ages

11:00 AM — Continue Over Wolf Creek Pass

  • Stop at the summit overlook (10,856 feet)
  • Views extend into New Mexico on clear days
  • Take photos at the Wolf Creek sign

Midday: Lunch in Pagosa

12:30 PM — Lunch Downtown

  • Option 1: Tequila’s Mexican (green chile smothered burrito)
  • Option 2: Kip’s Grill (fish tacos on the patio)
  • Option 3: Square Top BBQ (brisket, Thursday-Sunday only)

Afternoon: Choose Your Adventure

Option A: Hiking (Summer/Fall)

  • Easy: Reservoir Hill trails (downtown, 1-2 hours)
  • Moderate: Fourmile Falls (5 miles, waterfall reward)
  • Challenging: Continental Divide Trail sections

Option B: Skiing (Winter)

  • Wolf Creek Ski Area — 40 minutes away
  • “The Most Snow in Colorado”
  • Rent equipment on-site
  • Ski 9am-4pm

Option C: Relaxation (Any Season)

  • Overlook Hot Springs — Rooftop tubs with river views
  • Shopping downtown — Art galleries and boutiques
  • Pagosa Baking Company — Coffee and people-watching

Evening: Special Dinner

6:30 PM — Dinner at Meander Riverside Eatery

  • Upscale dining on the San Juan River
  • Reservations recommended
  • Perfect for celebrating your vacation
  • Try the Colorado lamb

9:00 PM — Nightcap Options

  • Option 1: Back to the cabin hot tub
  • Option 2: Coyote Moon Lounge for craft cocktails
  • Option 3: Stargazing from your deck (Gold-Tier Dark Sky!)

Day 3: Culture, Shopping & Departure

Morning: Chimney Rock National Monument

8:00 AM — Early Breakfast

  • Two Chicks & A Hippy (creative breakfast dishes)
  • Or Patria (farm-to-table)

9:30 AM — Drive to Chimney Rock

  • 45 minutes from Pagosa
  • Arrive when it opens (9 AM)
  • Allow 2-3 hours

What You’ll See:

  • Ancient Puebloan great house (1,000+ years old)
  • Twin rock formations (Chimney Rock and Companion Rock)
  • Petroglyphs
  • 360-degree mountain views

Tour Options:

  • Self-guided with brochure
  • Ranger-led tour (check schedule)
  • Full Moon Program (if staying overnight)

Midday: Downtown Exploration

12:30 PM — Lunch Downtown

  • Option 1: The Rose (brunch items until 2pm)
  • Option 2: Boss Hoggs (quick burgers)
  • Option 3: Rosie’s Pizzeria (NY-style slices)

1:30 PM — Shopping & Gallery Hopping

  • Main Street Gallery (local art)
  • The Artist’s Gallery (co-op, meet artists)
  • Wild West Gallery (Western art)
  • Browse boutiques and souvenir shops

Don’t Miss:

  • First Friday Art Walk (if visiting that weekend)
  • Public art and murals throughout downtown

Afternoon: Final Hot Springs Soak

3:30 PM — Healing Waters Resort

  • Budget-friendly option for your last soak
  • 100% natural mineral water
  • Less crowded than The Springs Resort
  • Perfect way to relax before departure

Evening: Departure

6:00 PM — Early Dinner (if leaving tonight)

  • Takeout option: Papa Murphy’s (take-and-bake pizza)
  • Quick sit-down: Dublin Crust & Brew
  • To-go option: Tequila’s (burritos travel well)

7:30 PM — Head Home

  • Or extend your stay — you won’t want to leave!

Alternative Day 3 Options

For Outdoor Enthusiasts

Replace Chimney Rock with:

  • Fly fishing on the San Juan River (Gold Medal Waters)
  • ATV/4x4 adventure on Continental Divide trails
  • Horseback riding through San Juan National Forest
  • Rock climbing at Penitente Canyon

For Wellness Seekers

Replace morning with:

  • Yoga class at Pagosa Springs Yoga Studio
  • Massage at The Springs Resort Spa
  • Forest bathing walk in the national forest
  • Meditation at your cabin

For Families

Kid-friendly adjustments:

  • Wolf Creek Wildlife Park (animals!)
  • Reservoir Hill (disc golf, sledding in winter)
  • Echo Canyon Reservoir (paddleboarding, beach)
  • Ice cream at Bliss Scoops

Packing Checklist

Essentials

  • Layers (mountain weather changes quickly)
  • Rain jacket (afternoon thunderstorms common)
  • Sunscreen and sunglasses (high altitude = intense UV)
  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Swimsuit (for hot springs)
  • Water bottle
  • Camera

Seasonal Additions

Summer:

  • Hat
  • Hiking boots
  • Bug spray

Winter:

  • Warm coat
  • Gloves and hat
  • Snow boots
  • Ski gear (or rent locally)

Dining Reservations to Make in Advance

Highly Recommended:

  • Meander Riverside Eatery — Book 2-3 days ahead
  • Full Moon Program at Chimney Rock — Book months ahead

Nice to Have:

  • The Springs Resort spa treatments — Book day before
  • Wolf Creek ski lessons — Book day before (winter)

Make It Your Own

This itinerary is a starting point — feel free to adjust based on your interests:

  • Add more hot springs time — You can never soak too much
  • Extend to 4-5 days — Add a day trip to Durango or Mesa Verde
  • Focus on one activity — Dedicate a day to fishing, skiing, or hiking
  • Slow down — Skip an activity and relax at the cabin instead

Book Your Stay

This itinerary is designed around staying at Pagosa Forest Lodge:

  • Private hot tub — Soak anytime, day or night
  • 5 private acres — Space to spread out and relax
  • Fully equipped kitchen — Cook meals when you want
  • 15 minutes to town — Close to everything
  • Pet-friendly — Bring your furry family members
