Best Restaurants in Pagosa Springs: Complete Local Dining Guide 2025

After a day of skiing at Wolf Creek or soaking in the hot springs, nothing hits the spot like a great meal. Pagosa Springs may be a small mountain town, but its dining scene punches well above its weight with over 40 restaurants, cafes, and eateries to choose from. From craft breweries and BBQ joints to authentic Mexican cuisine and cozy breakfast spots, Pagosa offers culinary experiences that rival much larger destinations.

The town’s location — surrounded by ranching country and close to the New Mexico border — creates a unique food culture that blends mountain comfort with Southwestern flavors. Local chefs take advantage of Colorado beef, fresh produce, and chile peppers that would make any New Mexican proud.

In this comprehensive dining guide, we’ll explore the best restaurants Pagosa Springs has to offer, organized by cuisine type and dining style. Whether you’re looking for a romantic dinner, family-friendly fare, or the perfect après-ski burger, you’ll find it here.

Fine Dining & Special Occasions

Alley House Grill

Hours: Tuesday-Sunday 4-9pm; Happy Hour Tues-Fri 4-5:30pm
Phone: (970) 264-0999

The Scene: Upscale dining in an intimate, historic setting

Alley House Grill offers refined American cuisine in a charming historic house. Known for their steak and seafood preparations, this is a top choice for special occasions or romantic dinners. The cozy atmosphere and attentive service make every meal feel like an event.

Known For:

  • Prime steaks — perfectly cooked to order
  • Fresh seafood — surprising quality for a mountain town
  • Craft cocktails — expertly mixed with premium spirits
  • Intimate ambiance — limited seating ensures quality experience

Meander Riverside Eatery

Hours: Wed-Sun 4pm-8:30pm (Bar opens earlier)
Phone: (970) 703-3244
Reservations: Recommended at meandereatery.com

The Scene: Upscale riverside dining with mountain views

For anniversary dinners, marriage proposals, or simply celebrating another perfect day in the mountains, Meander delivers an experience worthy of the occasion. The restaurant sits right on the San Juan River, with patio dining that feels special without being stuffy.

Perfect For: Celebrations, romantic dinners, special occasions

Menu Highlights:

  • Colorado lamb — locally sourced when available, perfectly prepared
  • Fresh seafood — surprisingly good for a landlocked mountain town
  • Craft cocktails — creative seasonal menus featuring local spirits
  • Oysters and caviar — featured on special occasions

Wine Program: Thoughtful selection balancing Colorado wines with international options

Reservations: Strongly recommended, especially for weekend evenings and holidays.

Colorado Roadhouse

Hours: Mon-Sat 11am-9pm; Closed Sunday
Phone: (970) 507-4800

The Scene: Classic American roadhouse with hearty portions

Colorado Roadhouse delivers generous portions of American comfort food in a casual, welcoming atmosphere. Their burgers, steaks, and comfort classics satisfy hearty appetites after outdoor adventures.

Cork & Board CO (Wine + Eats)

Hours: Wednesday-Saturday 2pm-8pm
Phone: (970) 744-2499

The Scene: Wine bar with elevated small plates

Cork & Board brings a sophisticated wine bar experience to Pagosa. Their carefully curated wine list pairs perfectly with their menu of creative small plates and charcuterie boards. It’s the perfect spot for a relaxed evening of wine and conversation.

Perfect For: Date nights, wine lovers, casual socializing

Craft Breweries & Casual Dining

Riff Raff Brewing Company

Hours: Daily 11am-9pm
Phone: (970) 264-6274

The Scene: Locals’ favorite brewpub housed in a beautifully restored historic downtown building

Riff Raff has become synonymous with Pagosa’s craft beer scene. Located in a building that dates back to the early 1900s, the brewery maintains original architectural features while creating a warm, welcoming atmosphere. The dog-friendly patio is perfect on sunny afternoons, and live music on weekends draws both locals and visitors.

Must-Try Menu Items:

  • Trout Creek Amber — their flagship brew, named after a local stream
  • Green Chile Cheeseburger — Colorado classic done right with locally sourced beef
  • Beer-battered fish and chips — using their own ale in the batter
  • Bison chili — hearty mountain fare perfect after a day outdoors

Why We Love It: Beyond the excellent beer and food, Riff Raff is committed to sustainable brewing practices. They were among the first in Colorado to install solar panels and use innovative water conservation techniques. Plus, it’s just 15 minutes from our luxury cabin, making it an easy dinner destination.

Pro Tip: Their downtown location makes it perfect for a stroll along the riverwalk after dinner.

The Break Room Brewing Company

Hours: Mon-Wed 11:30am-10pm, Thurs-Sun 11:30am-midnight
Phone: (970) 731-0566

The Scene: Industrial-chic brewery with a welcoming vibe

Newer to the Pagosa scene, The Break Room has quickly earned a loyal following with their creative brews and elevated pub food. The spacious interior features garage-door windows that open to the San Juan River during warmer months.

Standouts:

  • Rotating seasonal beers — their brewers love to experiment
  • Gourmet burgers — creative toppings and perfectly cooked patties
  • Shareable appetizers — great for groups
  • Late hours — one of the few spots open past 10pm

Mexican & Southwestern Cuisine

Phone: (970) 731-2501

The Scene: Authentic taqueria with fresh, flavorful tacos

Chavalo’s brings authentic Mexican street food to Pagosa. Their tacos are made with fresh ingredients and traditional preparations that honor Mexican culinary traditions. The casual setting makes it perfect for a quick, satisfying meal.

Phone: (970) 731-2454

The Scene: Family-friendly Mexican restaurant with generous portions

Tequila’s Mexican Restaurant

Hours: Daily 11am-10pm
Phone: (970) 264-9989

The Scene: Casual, family-owned authentic Mexican in a welcoming setting

Tequila’s serves the kind of Mexican food locals actually eat — authentic flavors that honor traditional recipes. The portions are generous, the prices are fair, and the margaritas are strong.

Order These:

  • Green chile smothered burritos — the true Colorado style
  • Street tacos — carne asada and al pastor are standouts
  • House margaritas — available by the glass or pitcher
  • Chile rellenos — house-made and perfectly executed

Local Secret: Ordering your food “Christmas” style (half red chile, half green chile) is the authentic Colorado way.

Dining Tips for Visitors: Making the Most of Pagosa’s Food Scene

Seasonal Considerations

SeasonDining Notes
WinterSome restaurants reduce hours; call ahead during off-peak weeks
SummerPatio dining is prime real estate — arrive early for outdoor tables
HolidaysBook reservations well in advance, especially Christmas through New Year
Mud Season (April-May)Slower pace, shorter waits, occasional closures for vacation
FallHarvest specials, pleasant patio weather

Reservation Strategies

  • Weekends: Book 2-3 days ahead for nicer restaurants like Meander
  • Meander: Reservations essential — book online at meandereatery.com
  • Large groups (6+): Always call ahead
  • Walk-ins: Most casual spots accept walk-ins, but expect waits during peak times

  • Rosie’s Pizzeria — pick up on your way home
  • Tequila’s — family feast packages perfect for groups
  • Square Top BBQ — grab smoked meats by the pound
  • Papa Murphy’s — take-and-bake convenience

Our fully equipped kitchen makes cabin dining easy and enjoyable. The large dining table seats 8 comfortably, and the outdoor grill extends your cooking options during warmer months.
